Annotated storyboard

Threadline turns complicated work into an executable plan.

Instead of pretending projects are just lists, it models dependencies, lane capacity, schedule risk, and uncertainty in one local-only planning instrument.

Deterministic scheduling Critical path Capacity-aware timeline Scenario diff Plain-English explanations

How to read it

1. Frame the work Set tasks, dependencies, lanes, deadlines, and uncertainty.
2. Watch the schedule emerge The timeline respects both prerequisites and lane capacity.
3. Interrogate the pressure points Bottlenecks and slip impact come from the same model, not separate guesses.
4. Compare futures Optimistic, expected, and conservative views show how uncertainty changes the finish.

Step 1

The timeline makes hidden queueing visible.

Work does not just wait on dependencies. It also waits for people and lanes to free up. Threadline shows both at once.

Capacity changes the truth. The plan is not just "A before B." It is "A before B, and B may still wait because the lane is busy."
The finish line is legible. The project finish, local deadlines, and critical tasks are visible without a giant spreadsheet.

Step 2

The diagnostics are explainable, not magical.

Bottlenecks, risk hotspots, and lane load all come from the same deterministic schedule model.

One-day slip impact The app can say which tasks matter because it recomputes the schedule, not because it vibes the answer.
Plain-English guidance The right rail translates the model into defer, cut, and leverage suggestions a human can act on.

Step 3

The dependency map exposes the shape of the project.

A plan feels hard when its structure is hidden. Threadline makes that structure compact and inspectable.

Depth comes from prerequisites. The map shows what must happen first, which is usually where schedule confidence actually comes from.
One model, many views. The same tasks drive the timeline, the diagnostics, and the graph instead of living in disconnected screens.

Step 4

Scenario mode keeps uncertainty explicit.

The point is not to predict the future perfectly. It is to show how sensitive the plan is when confidence gets worse or better.

Optimistic, expected, conservative Small changes in confidence can move the finish date. That is a signal to revisit scope or capacity, not to hope harder.
Takeaway: Threadline makes complexity feel juggled, not flattened, by showing how the work actually hangs together. Built from real local screenshots of the app.
