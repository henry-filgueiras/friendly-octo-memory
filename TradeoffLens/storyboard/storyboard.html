Annotated storyboard

Tradeoff Lens turns “which one feels right?” into a visible model.

The point is not to replace judgment. It is to force the decision into the open: what matters, how much it matters, what is a hard stop, and why the current leader is actually leading.

Step 1

Load a demo and get an immediate model, not an empty spreadsheet.

The left pane defines candidates and criteria. The center ranks them. The right explains the current result in normal language.

Demo-first onboarding The app is interesting immediately because real scenarios are one click away.
No black box The current leader, exclusions, and total active weight are visible at a glance.
Editable model Every candidate value, weight, and constraint can be changed in-place.

Step 2

Click a candidate and ask why it landed there.

The explanation pane surfaces what helped, what hurt, and what would need to change for an upset.

Pick a challenger Selecting Tesla reframes the right pane around a concrete “why not this one?” question.
Readable reasoning The explanation is derived from the scoring model, not pasted commentary.

Step 3

Use pairwise comparison to challenge a suspicious ranking.

If an option only wins because of weights, the pairwise matrix makes the tradeoffs legible fast.

Delta, not vibes Every matchup shows exactly how far apart two candidates are under the current weights.

Step 4

Switch to the Pareto frontier to see tradeoff shape, not just rank order.

This is the “show me the efficient options” view for two numeric criteria.

Frontier points Some candidates deserve attention because they are efficient tradeoffs even without being the top-ranked winner.

Step 5

Then drag the weights and see whether the ranking is robust or fragile.

If a small slider nudge changes the leader, that is valuable information about the decision itself.

Interactive stress test Sensitivity makes the ranking feel alive and reveals where the model is stable versus brittle.
Takeaway: Tradeoff Lens is compelling once people see that it explains itself at every step.
