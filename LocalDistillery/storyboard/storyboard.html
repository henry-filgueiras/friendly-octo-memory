Annotated storyboard

Local Distillery turns pasted text into a usable artifact fast.

The product works best when you understand the shape of the output immediately: not “an analyzer,” but a local distillation surface for turning raw material into a summary, digest, actions, questions, motifs, concepts, and an exportable result.

Static browser app No backend No telemetry Deterministic heuristics Markdown + JSON export

How to read it

Four quick moves

1
Start emptyThe interface accepts paste, drag-and-drop, or a demo sample.

3
Trim the artifactSection toggles let you keep only the output you want.
4
Export or copyThe result is meant to become a downstream artifact, not stay trapped in the UI.

Step 1

The empty state now explains the payoff before asking for input.

The old experience felt more like a blank utility. The improved first screen makes the demo path and the output shape much clearer.

Clear promise The product tells you what it does and what it does not do before you touch anything.
Guided first move A demo-first empty state reduces the “what am I supposed to paste here?” feeling.

Step 2

One source becomes a multi-part artifact.

The key value is visible transformation: summary, digest, actions, questions, patterns, concepts, and graph.

Immediate output The right pane proves the product quickly instead of asking users to imagine the result.

Step 3

The output is meant to be handled section-by-section.

Copy buttons, section toggles, and export controls make the artifact portable instead of ornamental.

Practical artifact This is less about “analysis for its own sake” and more about getting something reusable out of a messy input.

Step 4

The concept graph makes the heuristics feel inspectable, not magical.

It is a small thing, but showing graph structure and Mermaid source helps the app read as explainable local tooling rather than a mystery box.

Explainable structure The graph and source view signal how the tool thinks without pretending it is doing hidden semantic magic.
Takeaway: LocalDistillery felt clunky mostly because its output value was under-demonstrated, not because the core idea was weak.
