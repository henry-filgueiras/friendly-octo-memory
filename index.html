friendly-octo-memory

A small suite of local-only browser tools for making complicated things legible. The point is not to make complexity disappear. The point is to let you juggle it without turning the work into a giant table, a vague vibe, or a black box.

Choose your instrument

Four tools, one idea

Use the one that matches the shape of the problem. The suite is more convincing when the tools feel specific instead of pretending one interface can solve everything.

Text distillation

Local Distillery

Turn notes, transcripts, specs, logs, and research into a compact artifact with summary, digest, actions, questions, motifs, concepts, and export.

  • Best when the input is messy but the output should stay compact.
  • Feels like a local artifact machine, not a chat interface.

Evidence mapping

Evidence Ledger

Pressure-test claims against supporting evidence, contradiction, and missing sources so a clean narrative does not arrive earlier than the truth.

  • Best when the real problem is uncertainty hiding inside a tidy story.
  • Feels like an evidence instrument, not a note pile or a verdict machine.

Decision analysis

Tradeoff Lens

Compare candidates under explicit criteria, weights, and hard constraints with ranked results, pairwise views, frontier analysis, and plain-English explanations.

  • Best when the answer should be challengeable, not just felt.
  • Feels like an instrument panel instead of a spreadsheet.

Planning instrument

Turn complicated work into an executable plan by making dependencies, lane capacity, schedule risk, and uncertainty visible in one deterministic model.

  • Best when the real problem is coordination, not just task listing.

How they fit

One workflow, four surfaces

The suite works best when each tool gets to specialize. The handoff from one to the next is conceptual, not forced through a fake shared UI.

1. Understand

Distill the material

Start in Local Distillery when the problem is still buried in source notes, research, or logs and you need the artifact before the judgment.

2. Pressure-test

Challenge the narrative

Move to Evidence Ledger once the problem becomes which claims are truly supported, contradicted, contested, or still thin on evidence.

3. Choose

Make the tradeoffs explicit

Move to Tradeoff Lens once the real question is which option wins under stated values, weights, and hard constraints.

4. Land it

Map execution and pressure

Use Threadline when the decision is made and the challenge becomes dependency shape, queueing, deadlines, and schedule risk.

Why this is fun

The suite gets to show off a little

The interesting part is not that the apps are local-only. It is that they make complexity feel calm without pretending the underlying model is simple.

Local Distillery

Messy input becomes a working artifact

Instead of asking you to read chaos until clarity appears, it extracts the shape of the material directly into something you can use.

Evidence Ledger

Competing stories become legible

Instead of letting confidence arrive by momentum, it shows exactly where the evidence supports, contradicts, or simply runs out.

Tradeoff Lens

Subjective choices become inspectable

Instead of hiding the answer behind a score, it lets the score stay arguable and therefore useful.

Threadline

Projects become legible under pressure

Instead of flattening work into a list, it shows how dependencies and capacity actually steer the finish line.
